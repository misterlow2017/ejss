THE HIGHLIGHT APPROACH

STEP ONE: Sketch the figure

First, we represent the problem/situation as a simple drawing.

STEP TWO: Choose and isolate

Next, we choose one object for which the FBD will be drawn. We need a separate FBD for each object.

STEP THREE: Find and highlight the contact points

Determine all the contact points and/or surfaces that block A makes with other objects. Then, make a mental model to highlight all the contact points and surfaces for block A.

STEP Four: Identify and label contact forces

In the figure above, all the places of contact for block A are highlighted in yellow. The point of contact between the hand and block A, the applied force is directed towards the right. Between the contact surfaces of the block A and the table, the table exerts a normal reaction force on the block A. Between the contact surfaces of block A and block B, block B offers a resisting force on block A towards the left.

STEP FIVE: Draw the non contact forces

Determine all the non contact forces such as gravitational forces, magnetic force, etc that is acting on block A. In this case, the only non contact force acting on block A is the gravitational force.
