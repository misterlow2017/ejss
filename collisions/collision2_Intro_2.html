Suggestions on how to use this simulation

1. Before proceeding, you should ideally be familiar with the principles related to collition, namely, principle of conservation of momentum for al collisions. For elastic collisions, you will need to know about the principle of conservation of kinetic energy and how this leads to the equation relating the relative speed of approach and separation of the collision bodies.

2. For each simulation, try to predict the final speeds of the bodies before playing the simulation for the case of elastic and inelastic collisions.

3. Relate the graphs of the velocities to the bodies before, during and after the collision to your understanding of collisions. Recall that momentum is conserved at all times before, during and after collision and how that is represented in the graphs.

4. Relate the graphs of the accelerations of the bodies during the collision which is assumed to be constant for 0.05 sec. This is probably unrealistic, why? Is 0.05 sec realistic in a collision? Relate the acceleration to the force exerted by each body during the collision and recall Newton's 3rd Law.

5. What real life situations will result in an inelastic collision?
