At the end of this activity, students should be better able to describe and understand kinematic graphs of the motion of a body.

Constant velocity and zero acceleration

Positive velocity

Adjust the initial velocity to a positive value and set the acceleration setting to zero. Before playing the simulation, sketch the displacement-time, velocity-time and acceleraton-time graphs, taking positive sign as the rightward direction and the car to be initially at zero displacement.

 

1. Play the simulation and compare the displacement, velocity and acceleration graphs produced by the simulation with those you have sketched.

2. Note that the gradient of the displacement time graph at each time corresponds with the value on the velocity-time graph.

3. Note that the gradient of the velocity time graph at each time corresponds with the value on the acceleration-time graph.

4. Note that the area under the velocity-time graph is equal to the corresponding value on the displacement-time graph.

5. Note that the area under the acceleration-time graph is equal to the corresponding value on the velocity-time graph.

6. A common way of analyzing the motion of objects in physics labs is to perform a ticker tape analysis. This is represented as blue dots which are placed at 1 sec intervals. Observe how the separation between the dots changes, if any.

Negative velocity

Repeat the process as above but with a negative velocity value setting.
