This activity assumes that you are already familiar with the kinematics equations of motion.

For a particular setting of initial projection velocity and angle, acquaint yourself with the graphs of vertical and horizontal velocity, followed by the vertical and horizontal displacements. Relate the potential and kinetic energies to the horizontal displacement of the projectile.

For a particular setting, use the kinematics equations to predict the maximum height and the range of the projectile. At this stage, ignore the effect of air resistance.

For a particular setting, check the "Hole-in-Wall" game and keeping either velocity or angle fixed, predict and adjust the other variable and adjust the value. Confirm by playing and seeing the projectile pass through the hole.

Observe the effect of checking air resistance on the projectile motion. Specifically, observe the difference in the trajectory, changes to the maximum height and maximum range. Account for the differences in corresponding times to attain the maximum height and duration of the timings in the maximum range.
