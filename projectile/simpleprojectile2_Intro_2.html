The following activities assume that you are already familiar with the kinematics equations of motion and completed the linear motion of a body.

1. Vertical and horizontal graphical plots

For a particular setting of initial projection velocity and angle, sketch the vertical velocity and horizontal velocity graphs, paying attention to the axes values. Likewise, sketch the vertical and horizontal displacement graphs, again focussing on the axes and shape of the graphs. When you have completed the sketches, check against the graphical plots after you played the simulation.

2. Kinetic and potential energy plots

For a particular setting of initial projection velocity and angle, sketch the KE and PE with horizontal displacement. Compare your sketches with that produced by the simulation.

3. Maximum height and range of the projectile

For a particular setting, use the kinematics equations to predict the maximum height and the range of the projectile. At this stage, ignore the effect of air resistance. Compare your values with those obtained from the simulation.

4. Play the Hole-in-Wall game

For a particular setting, check the "Hole-in-Wall" game to show a wall with a hole on the screen. Keeping either velocity or angle fixed, predict and adjust the other variable to get the projectile to pass through the hole. This can be done by solving the kinematic equations in the vertical and horizontal directions. Confirm by playing and seeing the projectile pass through the hole.

5. Effect of air resistance on the projectile

Select the air resistance checkbox, and observe the effect of air resistance on the projectile motion. Specifically, observe the difference in the trajectory, changes to the maximum height and maximum range. Account for the differences in corresponding times to attain the maximum height and duration of the timings in the maximum range in terms of the changes to the acceleration in the vertical and horizontal direction.
