Two main forces acting on the skydiver of mass m are his weight and air resistance. The air resistance F can be written as follows

$$ F = \frac{1}{2} C_D\ A\ \rho\ v^2 $$ where \( \rho \) is the density of air, A is effective cross-sectional area and \( C_D \) is the drag coefficient.

(b)(i) Show that the terminal speed \(v_T\) that can be achieved by the skydiver as he falls is given by

$$ v_T = \sqrt{\frac{2mg}{C_DA\rho}} $$
At terminal speed, net force = 0

F – mg = 0

mg = \(\frac{1}{2} C_D\ A\ \rho\ v_T^2 \)
